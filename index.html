How Correlation is Calculated

The correlation analysis identifies which items commonly appear before or after your selected item/category, helping you discover patterns and potential relationships.

Analysis Steps

  1. Find all occurrences: The algorithm finds every instance of your selected item or category in the database.
  2. Single correlation per item: Each item in your data is evaluated relative to its closest target occurrence (by time distance). This prevents items between close target occurrences from being double-counted.
  3. Score based on timing: Items are scored according to their timing relative to their nearest target:
    • Positive correlation: Items appearing before the target within ~3 hours (or half the timeframe)‚Äîlikely contributing factors.
    • Neutral correlation: Items appearing before the target but beyond the threshold‚Äîweak or indirect relationships.
    • Negative correlation: Items appearing after the target (any distance within lookback)‚Äîcannot be causes, so they lower the correlation score.
  4. Rank results: Items are ranked by a correlation score from -1 (unlikely cause) to +1 (likely cause), calculated as: positive_ratio - negative_ratio
