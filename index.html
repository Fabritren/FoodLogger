How Correlation is Calculated

The correlation analysis identifies which items commonly appear before or after your selected item/category, helping you discover patterns and potential relationships.

Analysis Steps

  1. Find all occurrences: The algorithm finds every instance of your selected item or category in the database.
  2. Single correlation per item: Each item in your data is evaluated relative to its closest target occurrence (by time distance). This prevents items between close target occurrences from being double-counted.
  3. Classify by timing: Items are classified according to their timing relative to their nearest target:
    • Positive correlation: Items appearing before the target‚Äîlikely contributing factors.
    • Negative correlation: Items appearing after the target‚Äîcannot be direct causes.
    • Isolated targets: Target occurrences that this item does not explain (those where another item is closer).
  4. Calculate correlation score: Each item is scored using the weighted counts and user-defined gains:
    • Positive weight: Count of occurrences before target √ó Positive Gain
    • Negative weight: Count of occurrences after target √ó Negative Gain (typically negative to penalize after-occurrences)
    • Isolated weight: Count of unexplained targets √ó Isolated Target Gain (weight for targets this item doesn't explain)

    The score is calculated as: Score = (positiveWeight + negativeWeight + isolatedWeight) / totalCount, where totalCount is the sum of counts weighted by the absolute values of their corresponding gains: totalCount = positive √ó |gainPositive| + negative √ó |gainNegative| + isolated √ó |gainIsolated|. This ensures proper normalization regardless of gain magnitude. Higher (more positive) scores indicate likely causes; lower (more negative) scores indicate unlikely causes.
